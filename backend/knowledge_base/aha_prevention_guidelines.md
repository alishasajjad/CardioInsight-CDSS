# American Heart Association — Heart Disease Prevention (Educational Summary)

Source: Public health education materials (AHA-style guidance for learning purposes).

## Key Risk Factors
- High blood pressure (hypertension)
- High blood cholesterol
- Smoking and tobacco use
- Physical inactivity
- Overweight and obesity
- Diabetes
- Unhealthy diet high in saturated fat, trans fat, sodium, and added sugars

## Lifestyle Recommendations
1. **Eat smart:** Emphasize vegetables, fruits, whole grains, lean protein, and healthy fats. Limit sodium to support blood pressure control.
2. **Move more:** Adults should aim for at least 150 minutes per week of moderate-intensity aerobic activity or 75 minutes of vigorous activity.
3. **Maintain healthy weight:** BMI and waist circumference are associated with cardiovascular risk.
4. **Avoid tobacco:** Smoking dramatically increases coronary heart disease risk.
5. **Manage stress and sleep:** Chronic stress and poor sleep may contribute to hypertension and unhealthy behaviors.

## When to See a Doctor
- Persistent chest discomfort, pressure, or pain
- Shortness of breath with exertion
- Unexplained fatigue, dizziness, or palpitations
- Blood pressure consistently above 130/80 mmHg
- Total cholesterol or LDL above recommended targets

## Important Note
This content is for educational decision support only. Individual care plans must be determined by licensed clinicians.
