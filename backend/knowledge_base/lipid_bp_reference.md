# Lipid Management and Blood Pressure — General Educational Reference

## Cholesterol Targets (General Population Education)
- Desirable total cholesterol: often cited below 200 mg/dL in educational materials
- LDL ("bad" cholesterol): lower is generally better; targets depend on overall risk (clinical judgment)
- HDL ("good" cholesterol): higher associated with lower risk
- Triglycerides: elevated levels linked to metabolic syndrome and CVD risk

## Dietary Approaches
- Reduce saturated and trans fats
- Increase soluble fiber (oats, beans, fruits)
- Consider Mediterranean or DASH dietary patterns
- Limit processed foods and added sugars

## Blood Pressure
- Normal: typically < 120/80 mmHg (AHA categories may vary)
- Elevated / Stage 1 / Stage 2 hypertension require clinical evaluation
- Home blood pressure monitoring can aid tracking when validated devices are used

## Recommended Tests (Clinical Follow-up — Educational)
- Fasting lipid profile
- Blood pressure measurement (clinic and optionally home)
- Fasting glucose or HbA1c when metabolic risk present
- ECG when symptoms or risk factors warrant

Always interpret labs with a healthcare provider who knows your full history.
