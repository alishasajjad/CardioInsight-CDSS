# WHO — Cardiovascular Disease Overview (Educational)

Source: World Health Organization public information summary for educational use.

## Global Burden
Cardiovascular diseases (CVDs) are the leading cause of death worldwide. Most CVD deaths are due to heart attack and stroke.

## Major Modifiable Risk Factors
- Tobacco use
- Unhealthy diet and obesity
- Physical inactivity
- Harmful use of alcohol
- Raised blood pressure
- Raised blood glucose
- Raised blood lipids

## Prevention Strategies
Primary prevention focuses on reducing exposure to risk factors through population and individual interventions:
- Promote healthy diets low in salt, sugar, and saturated fats
- Increase physical activity across the lifespan
- Reduce tobacco exposure
- Detect and manage hypertension, diabetes, and dyslipidemia early

## Secondary Prevention
For people with established cardiovascular disease:
- Lifestyle modification plus evidence-based medical therapy as prescribed by clinicians
- Cardiac rehabilitation programs where available
- Regular follow-up for blood pressure, lipids, and glucose

## Disclaimer
WHO guidelines evolve; this summary supports educational AI retrieval only and does not replace official WHO publications or clinical judgment.
